140 Prompts for AI Infographics

English edition — every style, every directive, fully translated from the Swedish source.

This document collects all 140 art-direction prompts that produce the NotebookLM infographic styles on choosewise.education. Each prompt is numbered to match the original Swedish library and can be pasted directly into NotebookLM, Midjourney, Flux, or any other image generator that accepts long-form directives.

About this library

How to use these prompts

Each numbered entry below is a complete, stand-alone prompt. Copy the prompt in full and paste it into the image-generation field of your chosen tool. The numbering matches the Swedish library, so the same number produces the same style across languages — useful when you want to A/B the two wordings.

The prompts intentionally preserve English terms that were already anglicised in the Swedish source (glassmorphism, cel-shading, bokeh, HUD, bento grid, etc.) and all colour codes, font sizes, and proportions are kept as-is.

1 Style 001

Create a wide, horizontal "National Geographic-style editorial infographic spread" with a printed magazine feel: a light neutral background (white/off-white) with subtle paper grain, a strict grid and generous margins. At the top, place an elegant, large serif headline left-aligned, followed by a short deck in smaller type, and several small subheadings in small caps/sans-serif that divide the content into clear sections. The imagery should be a mix of photorealistic elements and natural-history/technical illustration: one large central hero motif (photograph or realistic painting) that functions as the "hero", complemented by 2–4 smaller inset figures (circles or rectangles with thin frames) showing magnifications, cutaways or small scene vignettes. Use thin grey annotation lines with dot anchors and discreet arrows connecting text blocks to specific details in the hero motif; keep labels short and precise and avoid thick lines. Include 1–2 clean data visualisations in classic editorial style (thin lines, small axes, discreet legend boxes), plus a small map/overview in one corner with muted colour fields and colour-coded markers. Typography should be highly readable and newspaper-like: serif headlines, body copy in small serif/sans with tight but airy leading, consistent columns, small figure numbers and captions; the colour palette should be sober with mostly black/dark-grey text and sparing accent colours (1–2 muted colours) used only to mark routes, arrows or key points. The overall impression should be a premium science spread: precise alignment, discreet separators, many small informative modules, a realistic hero motif, thin callouts and print-ready quality — no neon/HUD, no glassmorphism, and no hand-drawn sketchnote aesthetic.

Style rules: high detail, clean rendering, cinematic lighting, high contrast, clear silhouettes, no flat icons or hand-drawn lines, no low-resolution textures; everything should feel like a premium 3D-animated news poster with cyberpunk glow and a strong clickbait-thumbnail feel.
