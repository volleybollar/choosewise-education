Apple Intelligence Quick Start · for teachers 01 / 02

The six Apple Intelligence features that change classroom work.

iPad-first, privacy-aware, takes 10 minutes to learn.

Apple Intelligence runs mostly on your device — a genuine GDPR advantage when your work involves sensitive content. These six features give you the strongest daily return. Details on what leaves the device are in Part 3 of the full guide.

Six features, one pocket

1 Writing Tools

Rewrite, proofread, summarise. Grading comments, parent emails, lesson plans. Most on-device.

2 Siri 2.0

Context-aware scheduling, cross-app retrieval. On-device for nearly everything.

3 Mail categorisation

Primary / Transactions / Updates / Promotions. Find the email parents actually ask about.

4 Notification summaries

50 overnight alerts become one readable line. On-device, entirely.

5 Notes + Image Wand

Sketch or describe → polished illustration for worksheets and whiteboards.

6 Visual Intelligence

Point the camera at text or objects for translation, summaries, context.

5 things to try on your iPad today 02 / 02

Try it: 5 practical tasks.

Ten minutes, five moves, one less thing on your plate tonight.

1
Rewrite a parent email in three tonesWriting Tools offers Friendly / Professional / Concise. Pick the one that fits, send.
2
Summarise a 5-page curriculum PDFDrop the PDF into Notes, ask Writing Tools for a summary. Private Cloud Compute handles the long input.
3
Ask Siri for Thursday's schedule"Open Notes for Thursday's planning" and "Email the Year 9 parents about the trip" in one breath.
4
Triage a flooded inboxMail Primary shows the three parent questions that actually need answers; the rest is grouped quietly below.
5
Sketch a diagram in NotesRough circles and labels become a whiteboard-ready illustration via Image Wand.
● Watch out · age-gates and data

Some features are 18+; verify your national rules for student-facing use. De-identify documents before Apple Intelligence processes them. Disable ChatGPT integration on school devices by default — it sends text to OpenAI and opens a different privacy conversation entirely.
