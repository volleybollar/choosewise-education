Special Education Specialists

Five sample prompts from this prompt pack

Every prompt is a template with bracketed placeholders [replace this text] that you swap for your own context.

  1. Write a 200-word plan for how a special education specialist can adapt a mathematics lesson in [grade 4] for a student with ADHD, and provide 5 concrete strategies to support focus and engagement.
  2. Write a 200-word plan for how a special education specialist can support a student in [grade 5] with dyslexia in reading, and create 5 strategies to improve reading fluency and comprehension.
  3. Write a 200-word plan for how a special education specialist can support a student in [grade 5] with weak executive functions in planning their schoolwork, and provide 5 strategies to improve organisation.
  4. Create a 150-word text on how to use play-based learning to engage a student in [grade 1] with low motivation, and write 4 examples of play-based activities for different subjects. Behaviour Support and Classroom Management
  5. Create a 150-word text on how to involve parents in the follow-up of a student in [grade 1] with motor difficulties, and write 4 questions for discussing the student's progress with the parents.

How to use them

Paste the prompt into a chatbot. Where you see [bracketed text], replace it with what fits your situation. Always double-check output for accuracy — and never upload personal or sensitive data.

All prompts were originally developed in a Swedish school context. This means that some prompts may need to be adapted to the curriculum and context in your country.

These prompts pair with the WISE Framework for Education: the framework helps you decide whether to use an AI tool; the prompts help you use it well once you do.
