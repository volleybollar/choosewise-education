What's inside

Every prompt is written as a template with bracketed placeholders [like this] for you to swap in your own context. The set is split into two parts:

Part 1 · 70 prompts

General prompts for teachers

Planning, task design, classroom strategies, assessment, differentiation, collaboration, feedback. Works across subjects and age groups.

Part 2 · 50 prompts

Prompts linked to learning methods

Built around research-based techniques — spaced repetition, active recall, interleaved practice, dual coding, scaffolding, metacognition, feedback loops, chunking, elaborative interrogation, self-explanation, peer learning.

A few example prompts

  1. Create a lesson plan for a lesson in [subject] for students in [grade X]. The lesson is [50 minutes long] and I have 23 students.
  2. Give me an example of a differentiated task in [subject] for [grade] with three levels (basic, medium, advanced) that all connect to the same learning goal.
  3. Write a short guide for combining spaced repetition and active recall in a class in [grade X] in [subject] to review [specific unit, e.g. vocabulary], in three steps.

The full 120 prompts — along with a glossary, a prompt-writing framework, and short usage guidance — are in the downloadable PDF.

How to use them

Paste the prompt into a chatbot. Where you see [bracketed text], replace it with what fits your situation — a specific grade, subject, theme, or constraint. If the first response isn't quite right, iterate: add more context, adjust the role ("act as an experienced expert teacher in X"), or try a different chatbot. Always double-check output for accuracy — and never upload personal or sensitive data.

These prompts pair naturally with the WISE framework: the framework helps you decide whether to use an AI tool; the prompts help you use it well once you do.
